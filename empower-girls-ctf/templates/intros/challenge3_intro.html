Challenge 3: Substitution Cipher Decoder

What is a Substitution Cipher?

Ready to become a real code breaker? Substitution ciphers are one of the most powerful encryption methods used throughout history!

Unlike the Caesar cipher (which just shifts letters), a substitution cipher replaces each letter with a completely different letter. Every letter in the alphabet gets its own unique replacement, making it much harder to crack!

How It Works:

Each letter maps to a different letter, but not in a simple pattern!

Original Alphabet:
A B C D E F G H I J K L M
Cipher Alphabet:
Q W E R T Y U I O P A S D
Original: HELLO
Encrypted: ITSSO
Interactive Example:

Historical Secrets

Mary, Queen of Scots (1586)
Mary used substitution ciphers to send secret messages whilst imprisoned. Unfortunately for her, cryptanalysts cracked her code, revealing a plot against Queen Elizabeth I. This led to her execution! Substitution ciphers were serious business in history.

Frequency Analysis: Your Secret Weapon

How do you crack a substitution cipher? With frequency analysis! This is a real cryptography technique used by professional code breakers.

The Secret: In English, some letters appear more often than others!

  • Most Common: E, T, A, O, I, N, S, H, R
  • Least Common: Q, X, Z, J
  • Common Words: THE, AND, FOR, ARE
